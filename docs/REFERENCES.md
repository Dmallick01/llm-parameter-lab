# Research references

Educational widgets in LLM Parameter Lab are informed by (not full reimplementations of) the following work.

## KV cache & attention

- Vaswani, A., et al. (2017). [Attention Is All You Need](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762). NeurIPS.
- Dao, T., et al. (2022). [FlashAttention](https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.14135). NeurIPS.

## Quantization

- Jacob, B., et al. (2018). [Quantization and Training of CNNs for Efficient Integer-Arithmetic-Only Inference](https://arxiv.org/abs/1712.05877). CVPR.
- Frantar, E., et al. (2023). [GPTQ](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.17323). arXiv.

## Sampling

- Holtzman, A., et al. (2020). [The Curious Case of Neural Text Degeneration](https://arxiv.org/abs/1904.09751). ICLR.
- Fan, A., et al. (2018). [Hierarchical Neural Story Generation](https://arxiv.org/abs/1805.04833). ACL.

## RLHF / alignment

- Ouyang, L., et al. (2022). [Training language models to follow instructions with human feedback](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.02155). NeurIPS.
- Rafailov, D., et al. (2023). [Direct Preference Optimization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.18290). NeurIPS.

## RAG

- Lewis, P., et al. (2020). [Retrieval-Augmented Generation for Knowledge-Intensive NLP Tasks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.11401). NeurIPS.

## Scaling laws

- Kaplan, J., et al. (2020). [Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.08361). arXiv.
- Hoffmann, J., et al. (2022). [Training Compute-Optimal Large Language Models (Chinchilla)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.15556). NeurIPS.
- Brown, T., et al. (2020). [Language Models are Few-Shot Learners (GPT-3)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.14165). NeurIPS.
